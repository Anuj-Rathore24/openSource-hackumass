Are you interested in helping run an annual event which helps nearly 1,000 students learn about and build with technology? If so, apply to be an organizer for next year's HackUMass!

For the past seven years, HackUMass has grown to become the largest student-run technology event on campus -- and the second largest student-run event overall besides Homecoming. We are looking for students interested in volunteering their time to help prepare for HackUMass 8, which will occur next fall.

No matter your interests or background, there are a variety of different roles which we are looking to fill in our organizing team. Whether it's helping plan out the logistics of the event and coordinating with sponsors, or running our hardware inventory and building technology tools to help run the event, applications for our organizing team are now open!

Hello there! Thanks for your interest in applying to be a HackUMass organizer. We have opened applications for positions which work exclusively on the team which organizes HackUMass, as well as positions on the joint team which manages both HackUMass and HackHer413 (also known as FUSE).

The HackUMass Sponsorship Team is in charge of the finances for the event, obtained through company sponsorships. They plan out our sponsorship tiers, outreach strategy, and are in regular contact with potential sponsors. Closer to the event, the sponsorship team focuses on sponsorship-related logistics, ensuring that all of the companies and mentors attending the event know everything they need to help the event.

The HackUMass Public Relations Team is in charge of student and university outreach. This includes running social media accounts, planning giveaways, and representing HackUMass at on-campus events to boost student recruitment. The PR team also creates materials such as flyers and posters which are used to promote the event.

The HackUMass Logistics Team is in charge of all other logistics which allow the event to happen, from booking the venue and deciding an event date, to working with UMass Dining to order food, to booking buses for non-UMass students to attend the event, to designing a robust day-of schedule to keep the event on track. Without people on the Logistics Team, there would be no venue, no prizes, and no food!

The FUSE Tech Team works with both HackUMass and HackHer413 works on applications such as Dashboard (https://github.com/fuseumass/dashboard) and HackerForce (https://github.com/fuseumass/hackerforce) which are both open source and used by other hackathons. We also build out the HackUMass and Hack(H)413 websites as well as perform other technology and infrastructure tasks.

The FUSE Hardware Team works with both HackUMass and HackHer413 on managing our extensive inventory of hardware, providing insight into what role hardware technologies should play in our hackathons, and performing other logistical tasks relating to hardware. The Hardware Team is closely affiliated with M5, the Engineering Makerspace, as well as the All-Campus Makerspace.

The FUSE Design Team is a new team which will work with both HackUMass and HackHer413 on design work, ranging from social media branding, to creating flyers and other promotions, to working with the Tech team on UI/UX designs for websites and web applications, to (potentially) light video editing.

The exact tasks that you'll be assigned will depend on your experience and comfort level, so no worries if you don't have experience in all of those areas!

If you have any questions and you're not sure who to direct them to, please email team@hackumass.com!
